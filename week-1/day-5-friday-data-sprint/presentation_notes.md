## 1. What dataset did we analyze?

We analyzed an orders dataset that contains information about customers, cities, products, categories, quantities, prices, order status, and order dates.

## 2. What did we do in Python?

In Python, we loaded and cleaned the data. We checked the dataset structure, handled data formatting, calculated new values, and prepared the data for analysis.

## 3. What did we do in SQL?

In SQL, we created the orders table, inserted the data, and wrote queries to analyze the orders. We filtered completed and cancelled orders, calculated revenue, grouped data by city and category, and found the most valuable orders.

## 4. What was our most important business insight?

Our most important insight was that completed orders are the only ones that should count as revenue. We also identified the products and categories that generate the highest value.

## 5. What was difficult?

The most difficult part was making sure the data was clean and that the SQL queries matched the correct columns and values in the dataset.

## 6. What would we improve if we had more time?

If we had more time, we would create visual dashboards, add more data analysis, and build automated data pipelines for faster reporting.
