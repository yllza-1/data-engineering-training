# Data Pipeline Thinking - Day 3

## Chosen business:

Gym Membership Management

## Source Data:

The source data comes from gym membership forms, payment records, and member visit check-ins.
This data includes member information, membership plans, prices, visits, and membership status.

## Ingestion:

The data is collected from gym systems and entered into a database through applications or manual uploads.

## Storage:

The data is stored in database tables, such as the memberships table, where member details and activity information are organized.

## Cleaning:

Cleaning removes incorrect or missing data. For example, fixing missing member names, correcting invalid statuses, and removing duplicate records.

## Transformation:

The data can be transformed by calculating total income, counting active members, and analyzing visits by membership plan.

## Business Output:

The final output can be reports and dashboards that show active members, revenue information, and membership performance.

## Business questions we can answer:

1. How many active members does the gym have?
2. Which membership plan generates the highest income?
3. Which members have the most visits?

## Possible data problems:

1. Missing or incorrect member information.
2. Duplicate membership records.
3. Incorrect payment or status data.
